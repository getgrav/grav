## Writing Guidelines

### Tone & Voice
- **Professional** but friendly and approachable
- **Confident** in promoting Ogle County
- **Factual** - back up claims with data
- **Action-oriented** - encourage contact and engagement
- **Inclusive** - welcoming to all businesses

### Style Guidelines
- **Avoid jargon** - explain technical terms
- **Active voice** - "We help businesses grow" not "Businesses are helped by us"
- **Short paragraphs** - 2-4 sentences for web readability
- **Bullet points** - for lists and features
- **Headers** - break up long content
- **Call-to-actions** - encourage next steps

### SEO Best Practices
- **Keywords** - Include relevant terms naturally
  - Ogle County
  - Economic development
  - Illinois business
  - Site selection
  - Business incentives
- **Meta descriptions** - 150-160 characters per page
- **Headers (H1, H2, H3)** - Logical structure
- **Alt text** - Describe all images
- **Internal links** - Link between relevant pages
- **External links** - Link to authoritative sources

### Legal & Compliance
- [ ] Verify all statistics are current and accurate
- [ ] Source data properly
- [ ] Ensure incentive information is up-to-date
- [ ] Comply with accessibility guidelines (WCAG 2.1 AA)
- [ ] Include required disclaimers (if any)
- [ ] Respect copyright on images and content

## Content Review Process

### Before Publishing
1. **Accuracy check** - Verify all facts and figures
2. **Spell check** - No typos or grammar errors
3. **Link check** - All links work
4. **Image check** - All images load and display properly
5. **Mobile check** - Content readable on mobile
6. **SEO check** - Meta tags, headers, keywords
7. **Accessibility check** - Alt text, proper contrast
8. **Legal review** - Compliance and disclaimers

### Content Approval
- [ ] OCEDC Executive Director approval
- [ ] Marketing/Communications review
- [ ] Legal review (if required)
- [ ] Final client sign-off

## Content Delivery

### How to Provide Content

**Preferred Formats**:
- **Text**: Microsoft Word (.docx) or Google Docs
- **Images**: JPG (photos), PNG (logos/graphics), SVG (icons/logos)
- **Spreadsheets**: For data tables (sites, contacts, etc.)
- **Organized folders**: By page or content type

**Naming Conventions**:
- Descriptive filenames
- Lowercase, hyphens (not spaces)
- Examples:
  - `hero-ogle-county-aerial.jpg`
  - `team-john-smith.jpg`
  - `site-rochelle-industrial-park.jpg`

**Submission Methods**:
- Email (for small files)
- Google Drive share
- Dropbox share
- WeTransfer (for large files)
